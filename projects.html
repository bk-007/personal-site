Selected work

Selected work across operations, quality, people, and technology.

My work usually begins in the gap between how an operation is supposed to run and what is actually happening. These examples show how I use leadership, process improvement, quality systems, data, and practical technology to close that gap.

The examples are intentionally general because the underlying manufacturing data and internal processes are confidential.

01 / PEOPLE SYSTEM
Team development and leadership

Building a capable, accountable quality team

I built a stronger team culture through clear expectations, regular one-on-ones, meaningful recognition, cross-training, and delegated ownership. The goal was not simply to get more work done. It was to help people make sound decisions, take pride in their work, and grow into greater responsibility.

Stronger ownershipPeople trusted with meaningful responsibility
Greater engagementGood work noticed and development made visible
More capacityCapability expands beyond the supervisor
Coaching · Delegation · Performance management · Employee development
02 / CROSS-FUNCTIONAL LEADERSHIP
Talent development beyond the org chart

Seeing potential and giving it somewhere to go

Some of my most useful leadership work began before anyone formally assigned it to me. I have coached and developed people outside my reporting line by giving them real operational problems, room to test their own ideas, and support without taking the work back from them.

Earlier contributionPotential turned into useful work quickly
Practical learningDevelopment built around real problems
Cross-functional trustLeadership created without relying on title
Mentoring · Delegation · Applied learning · Influence without authority
03 / DATA SYSTEM
Manufacturing intelligence

Putting scattered production data in one place

Production and test data was scattered across files, folders, and disconnected systems. I built an application that consolidated the information, linked related work orders, compared runs, calculated key metrics, and generated usable reports.

One source of truthReplaced fragmented file hunting
Faster analysisSearch and compare in minutes
Better decisionsConnects process and test history
Python · SQLite · Data pipelines · Desktop UI · Reporting
04 / QUALITY SYSTEM
Inspection and traceability

Making quality information easier to use

I connected inspection records, measurement data, work-order history, dashboards, and shared resources into a more accessible quality system. The result reduced dependence on tribal knowledge and made information easier for nontechnical users to find and apply.

Less manual workAutomated repetitive preparation
Stronger traceabilityRecords linked across workflows
Wider accessBuilt for nontechnical users
SQL · Power BI · SharePoint · Excel · Process design
05 / AUTOMATION
AI-assisted operations

Using AI without pretending it is magic

I built internal AI assistants and small tools for problem solving, document guidance, data cleanup, reporting, and routine analysis. I treat AI as a practical support tool: define the task clearly, verify the output, and keep human judgment in the process.

Reusable knowledgeGuidance available at the point of work
Faster iterationIdeas move quickly into prototypes
Human oversightTools support, not replace, judgment
Python · Microsoft 365 · Copilot · Prompt systems · Automation
06 / PEOPLE SYSTEM
Training and leadership

Giving people a real path to grow

I created competency levels, cross-training paths, and clearer expectations so employees could understand their current capability and what came next. The approach emphasized hands-on learning, guided problem solving, and increasing responsibility over time.

Visible growthClearer capability progression
Distributed knowledgeLess dependence on one expert
Greater ownershipPeople learn through doing
Competency design · Coaching · Standard work · Change leadership
07 / PROCESS IMPROVEMENT
Manufacturing systems

Fixing the point where the process actually breaks

I have led improvements across measurement methods, machine ownership, incoming inspection, defect containment, testing, materials, and cross-functional handoffs. I start by observing the work, involving the people closest to it, and identifying where the actual process differs from the intended one.

Earlier detectionProblems found before downstream escape
Clearer ownershipProcesses assigned and maintained
Cross-functional actionQuality connected to operations
Lean Six Sigma · Root cause analysis · Metrology · Manufacturing
08 / RAPID DEVELOPMENT
Physical and digital prototypes

Turning “someone should fix that” into a real solution

I use 3D printing, scanning, fabrication, software, and frontline feedback to move quickly from an operational problem to a testable solution. Early prototypes create a faster feedback loop and allow the people using the solution to shape the next version.

Shorter feedback loopsPrototype, test, revise
Frontline inputUsers shape the solution
Lower frictionSmall problems stop becoming permanent
3D printing · CAD collaboration · Scanning · Prototyping

The best improvements connect people, process, and technology.

Technology can make an improvement possible, but clear ownership, capable people, and a process designed around the real work are what make it last.
